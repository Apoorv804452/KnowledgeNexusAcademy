üîπ 1. Balanced and Unbalanced Forces

Force is a push or pull. It can change speed, direction, or shape of an object. But multiple forces may act together.

  • Balanced forces: equal in magnitude, opposite in direction ‚Üí net force = 0 ‚Üí no change in state of rest/uniform motion.
  • Unbalanced forces: net force ‚â† 0 ‚Üí motion changes (accelerates).
Activity 8.1 (NCERT): Tie two strings to a wooden block. Pull from both sides equally ‚Üí block doesn‚Äôt move (balanced). Pull harder on one side ‚Üí block moves towards greater force (unbalanced).

Example ‚Äì friction: When children push a box on rough floor:

  • small push ‚Üí friction balances ‚Üí no motion.
  • push harder ‚Üí still balanced until max friction.
  • push even harder ‚Üí unbalanced ‚Üí box moves.

üìå Friction opposes motion, but an object moves with uniform velocity when forces are balanced (no net force).

üîπ 2. First Law of Motion (Law of Inertia)
Statement: An object remains in a state of rest or of uniform motion in a straight line unless compelled to change that state by an applied unbalanced force.

Inertia: natural tendency of objects to resist a change in their state of rest or motion.

üìê Galileo‚Äôs inclined plane thought experiment

  • Marble rolling down ‚Üí speed increases.
  • Marble rolling up ‚Üí speed decreases.
  • On a frictionless double incline, marble would rise to same height.
  • If second slope is horizontal ‚Üí marble would keep moving forever (ideal).

‚û°Ô∏è Conclusion: No net force needed to sustain uniform motion; only to change it.

Activity 8.2 (NCERT): Pile of carom coins. Hit the bottom coin hard with striker ‚Äì bottom coin moves out, rest fall vertically due to inertia of rest.
Activity 8.3 (NCERT): Coin on card over glass. Flick card sharply ‚Äì coin drops into glass (inertia of rest).

üöó Real life examples:

  • When bus suddenly stops, passengers fall forward (inertia of motion).
  • When bus starts suddenly, passengers fall backward (inertia of rest).
  • Seat belts ‚Äì provide force to slow down our motion, preventing injury.
üîπ 3. Inertia and Mass

Inertia is directly related to mass. Heavier objects have more inertia ‚Äì they resist change more strongly.

  • Empty box pushed easily ‚Üí less inertia.
  • Box full of books harder to push ‚Üí more inertia.
  • Kicking a football vs kicking a stone of same size ‚Äì stone has more mass ‚Üí more inertia.

‚úÖ Mass is the measure of inertia. SI unit: kilogram (kg).

üîπ 5. Third Law of Motion
Statement: To every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction. They act on two different bodies.
  • Forces always occur in pairs (action‚Äìreaction).
  • Equal in magnitude, opposite in direction.
  • Act on different objects, so they don‚Äôt cancel each other.
Activity 8.4 (NCERT): Two spring balances connected. Pull one ‚Äì both show same reading. Force on A by B = force on B by A.

Examples:

  • Gun recoil: bullet forward force ‚Üí gun backward (recoil).
  • Rocket propulsion: exhaust gases downward ‚Üí rocket upward.
  • Jumping from boat: you jump forward, boat moves backward.

‚ö†Ô∏è Even though forces are equal, accelerations differ (masses differ).
